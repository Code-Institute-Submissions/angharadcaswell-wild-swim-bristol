There are beautiful swim spots in and around Bristol. Check out our 5 favourite places to wild swim.

1. Clevedon Marine Lake


Clevedon Marine Lake is a tidal infinity pool filled with sea water. It is a very popular spot with beautiful views of the pier and the Bristol Channel.

It is regularly drained and monitored to maintain excellent water quality.

pool is the perfect way to start your wild swimming journey as it has easy access to the water and shallower sections. You will see a range of swimming abilities at the Clevedon. From people swimming lengths to those who just want to float around and take in the views. There is also a shallow and contained section for children to swim in.


Best way to enjoy the pool... avoid the crowds by visiting in the evening for a stunning sunset swim followed by some tasty fish & chips.


Location

  • You can cycle to Clevedon from Bristol. It requires a good level of fitness and confidence to ride on the roads. Check out this route on Komoot.

  • Walking to Clevedon from Bristol is also a great option for those who like to take a more adventerous journey. Hike via Cadbury Camp fort and reward yourself for those miles with a dip in the lake at the end. To get back either reverse the route or take a public bus back into Bristol town. Check out this Komoot route.

  • Another green option is to take the public bus from Bristol to Clevedon. Here is the bus timetable.

  • Clevedon is located a 40 minute drive from Bristol. Drive to Clevedon and follow signs to the Marine Lake. There is paid parking at the lake or you can find free street parking nearby, however this is limited.

  • Address:

    170 Old Church Rd, Clevedon BS21 7TU

2. Warleigh Weir


Warleigh Weir is located closer to Bath than Bristol but is a very popular spot for any wild swimmer. The disadvantages of this spot is it can get very busy in the summer and there is no official parking. We think the latter is a great opportunity to use alternative transport options if they are available to you. See below for our recommendations.

Once you have arrived at the Weir there is easy access into the water (small steps) and a large area of grass to bask in the sun after your swim.

It is private land and needs to be respected. You can’t have BBQs and there are no amenities. Make sure you have everything you need for a fun day of picnics and swimming.

Warleigh Weir is campaining to be an official Designated Swimming Spot. This means that the water quality would be regularly checked. You can help make this happen, take a look at the information provided here.

Location

  • Bath where you take a short ride to the Weir. There is only a short amount of time on main roads and the rest is on the bike path. It is a great option for all abilities. If you would prefer not to cycle back you could take a train from Bath center. Check out this route on Komoot.

  • You can also walk the cycling route. Check out this Komoot route.

  • Take the train from Bristol to Bath Spa. Direct trains can take as little as 12 minutes to get to Bath. From there you have the option to a 4 mile walk or cycle to the weir Route here. If you choose to cycle, don’t forget to book your bike onto the train. Or you can take the bus (14 mins) to Claverton Village (D1 service towards North Bradley). Route can be viewed on the First Bus website.

  • Address:

    Ferry Ln, Claverton, Bath BA2 7BH

3. Saltford


Saltford swim spot is located just off the Bristol/ Bath bike path. There are access points along both sides of the river. Be careful as it gets deep quickly and boats use the stretch of water. A tow float is definitely recommended so you are visible in the water.


There are no shops near the spot so make sure you have everything you need for a fun day out. The Jolly Sailor pub has a garden next to the weir for your post-swim pint.

Location

  • This is a great cycle route as the majority is on the Bristol/ Bath bike path. It’s 10 miles out to Saltford but on a safe and pretty much flat route. You can also stop off at cafes on the way if you need a break. Route here on Komoot .

  • Walking is another option for a big day out. Walk along the river path from Bristol to Saltford. If you don’t want to walk back, you could continue into Bath after your swim and get the train. Route here.

  • Bus services are avilable to Saltford. Check out the timings here.

  • Parking is available and free but limited. We would recommend using the above methods of transport.

  • Address:

    Mead Ln, Saltford, Bristol BS31 3ER

4. Pensford Viaduct


Visit Pensford to swim right next to the Viaduct. The pool is overall pretty shallow so great for a dip and build your confidence. You can get into the river further round if you want a longer swim.


Good access to the water down the bank and plenty of green space to enjoy before/after your swim.


There is a great and very friendly pub right next to the swim spot. The Rising Sun pub is perfect for a tasty lunch after your swim.


To reach the swim spot, you take the walking bridge to the right on the pub.
