There are beautiful swim spots in and around Bristol. Check out our 5 favourite places to wild swim.

1. Clevedon Marine Lake


Clevedon Marine Lake is a tidal infinity pool filled with sea water. It is a very popular spot with beautiful views of the pier and the Bristol Channel.

It is regularly drained and monitored to maintain excellent water quality.

The pool is the perfect way to start your wild swimming journey as it has easy access to the water and shallower sections. You will see a range of swimming abilities at the Clevedon. From people swimming lengths to those who just want to float around and take in the views. There is also a shallow and contained section for children to swim in.

Best way to enjoy the pool... avoid the crowds by visiting in the evening for a stunning sunset swim followed by some tasty fish & chips.

2. Warleigh Weir


Warleigh Weir is located closer to Bath than Bristol but is a very popular spot for any wild swimmer. The disadvantages of this spot are it can get very busy in the summer and there is no official parking. We think the latter is a great opportunity to use alternative transport options if they are available to you. See below for our recommendations.

Once you have arrived at the Weir there is easy access into the water (small steps) and a large area of grass to bask in the sun after your swim.

It is private land and needs to be respected. You canâ€™t have BBQs and there are no amenities. Make sure you have everything you need for a fun day of picnics and swimming.

Warleigh Weir is campaigning to be an official Designated Swimming Spot. This means that the water quality would be regularly checked. You can help make this happen, take a look at the information provided here.

3. Saltford


Saltford swim spot is located just off the Bristol/ Bath bike path. There are access points along both sides of the river. Be careful as it gets deep quickly and boats use the stretch of water. A tow float is definitely recommended so you are visible in the water.


There are no shops near the spot so make sure you have everything you need for a fun day out. The Jolly Sailor pub has a garden next to the weir for your post-swim pint.

4. Pensford Viaduct


Visit Pensford to swim right next to the Viaduct. The pool is relatively shallow overall so it is great for a dip and to build your confidence. You can get into the river further round if you want a longer swim.


Good access to the water down the bank and plenty of green space to enjoy before/after your swim.


There is a great and very friendly pub right next to the swim spot. The Rising Sun pub is perfect for a tasty lunch after your swim.


To reach the swim spot, you take the walking bridge to the right of the pub.
